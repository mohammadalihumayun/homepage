Experience

Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the Information Technology University (ITU), Lahore, Pakistan. I supervise research regarding speech, audio, language, and biomedical signal processing with deep learning. Besides, I teach statistics and signal processing courses and have designed an elective course for ‘Speech Signal Processing’. Before being appointed as Assistant Professor, I worked as a lecturer for a year in the same department.

Projects

Leading a research project involving the curation of a Phonocardiogram (PCG) dataset comprising Malaysian and UK populations, designing a novel algorithm for unsupervised anomaly detection from PCG, interpreting anomaly detection using self-explanatory models, and developing an online mobile application for real-time PCG audio diagnosis.

Other notable projects include fine-tuning Transformer models for language sentence classification tasks and developing a constrained autoencoder for unsupervised representation learning in speech.

Publications

Published several papers focusing on deep learning for speech and language processing. Notable contributions include exploring optimal time window durations for short-term spectral speech features regarding accent classification, developing a constrained autoencoder for unsupervised representation learning in speech, and proposing a novel strategy for fine-tuning Transformer models for linguistic dialect classification tasks.

Education

PhD in machine learning from Universiti Brunei Darussalam (UBD), with research focused on deep learning for speech and language processing. Made several novel contributions in the area, including exploring optimal time window durations for short-term spectral speech features regarding accent classification and developing a constrained autoencoder for unsupervised representation learning in speech.
